Frequently Asked Questions

What is powerlifting?

Powerlifting is a strength sport that consists of three main disciplines: squat, bench press, and deadlift. The goal is to lift the heaviest weight possible in each discipline.

Who can train powerlifting?

Powerlifting is for everyone! Regardless of age, gender, or experience level, our club tailors the training to each individual to help you achieve your goals.

How does powerlifting impact my body?

Powerlifting builds strength, improves muscle and bone density, and enhances your body composition, making you stronger and more resilient.

Can powerlifting help with weight loss?

Yes, powerlifting can aid in weight loss as lifting heavy weights burns calories and boosts metabolism, even after the workout.

Is powerlifting good for older individuals?

Definitely. Powerlifting strengthens muscles and bones, which is crucial for maintaining mobility and reducing injury risks in older adults.

How often should I train?

It depends on your goals and schedule. Typically, 3-5 training sessions per week are recommended for optimal progress.

What are the age categories in powerlifting?

Powerlifting includes several age categories:

  • Sub-Junior: Up to 18 years old
  • Junior: 19-23 years old
  • Senior/Open: 24-39 years old
  • Masters: 40+ years old (further divided into subcategories: Masters I, II, III, etc.)

What are the weight categories in powerlifting?

Weight categories include:

  • Men 59 kg, 66 kg, 74 kg, 83 kg, 93 kg, 105 kg, 120 kg, 120+ kg
  • Women: 47 kg, 52 kg, 57 kg, 63 kg, 69 kg, 76 kg, 84 kg, 84+ kg

How do I sign up for a competition?

You can sign up through the federation or the competition organizer. It’s important to note that you must be a club member to compete.

What equipment do I need for competitions?

To compete, a competition singlet, cotton t-shirt, and long socks for deadlifts are mandatory. Other equipment, such as belts, knee sleeves, and wrist wraps, is optional but can be used for additional support or safety.

When does the weigh-in take place?

The weigh-in takes place 2 hours before the competition begins. It’s a mandatory process to determine your weight category. After the weigh-in, you’ll have enough time to eat, rehydrate, and get ready for the competition.

What does the club’s motto 'Club Above All' mean?

The motto 'Club Above All' reflects our philosophy that no individual is more important than the club. All members support one another, cheer each other on during training and competitions, and work together to become the best athletes they can be. The strength of our club lies in its unity.
